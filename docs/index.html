Margin

  • margin all: .ma*[-lg/-sm]
  • margin horizontally (left & right): .mh*[-lg/-sm]
  • margin vertically (top & bottom): .mv*[-lg/-sm]
  • margin bottom: .mb*[-lg/-sm]
  • margin top: .mt*[-lg/-sm]
  • margin left: .ml*[-lg/-sm]
  • margin right: .mr*[-lg/-sm]
* = -auto, 0 (0 rem), 025 (0.25rem), 050 (0.50rem), 100 (1.00rem), 150 (1.50rem), 300 (3.00 rem)

For example,

  • .ma150 creates 1.50rem margin on all sides on all devices.
  • .ma100-lg creates 1rem margin on all sides on large devices.
  • .ma075-sm creates 0.75rem margin on all sides on small devices.

Minimum to content

For text content this means that the content will take all soft-wrapping opportunities, becoming as small as the longest word.

Classes are .mincontent, .mincontent-sm, .mincontent-lg

Fig: Div without .mincontent

Maximum to content

The max-content sizing keyword represents the intrinsic maximum width or height of the content. For text content this means that the content will not wrap at all even if it causes overflows.

Classes are .maxcontent, .maxcontent-sm, .maxcontent-lg
